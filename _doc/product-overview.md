# Product Overview

> Single source of truth for the product. Branding marked *(provisional)* is a working
> assumption pending the founder's confirmation, not a locked decision.

## What it is

A **customer onboarding app** for an early-stage startup: the operational tool the team
uses to take a new customer from "just signed up" to "actively using the product." It
tracks where each customer is in onboarding and nudges the ones who stall with email
reminders, so fewer new customers go cold before they reach first value.

**Name:** FirstMile — named for the customer's "first mile," the stretch from signup to
first value where activation is won or lost.

## Who it's for

- **Primary users — the startup's onboarding team:** founders, customer success, and
  account managers who log in to manage and track new customers through onboarding.
- **Recipients — the new customers being onboarded:** they don't use the app directly;
  they receive the email reminders the team sends/schedules from it.

## The problem

In a young startup, new signups quietly stall mid-onboarding and never reach first
value. Without a single place to see who is stuck and a reliable way to nudge them,
those customers churn before they ever become real, paying users — and that lost
activation is invisible until the revenue doesn't show up.

## The solution

One focused workspace where the team can see every customer's onboarding status at a
glance and trigger email reminders to move stalled customers forward — turning onboarding
from an ad-hoc, forgettable manual chore into a tracked, repeatable process.

## Core workflow (the operation this app exists to perform)

1. A team member logs in.
2. The dashboard shows onboarding status across all customers (who's progressing, who's stalled).
3. They open a customer record to view/update that customer's onboarding details and progress.
4. They send or schedule an **email reminder** to nudge a stalled or upcoming customer.

The **verified operations** this app lives by:
- **Onboarding reminder sent** — an email reminder dispatched to a customer.
- **Customer onboarding record advanced** — a customer moved forward a step / marked active.

## MVP scope (v1)

- **User login** — authenticated access for the team.
- **Dashboard** — overview of customers and their onboarding status.
- **Customer records** — create, view, and update customer onboarding records.
- **Email reminders** — send/schedule reminder emails to customers.

Out of scope for v1: billing, in-product (in-app) onboarding flows for the end customer,
multi-team permissions, analytics dashboards, integrations. Candidates for later.

## Positioning, tone & principles

- **Positioning:** a lightweight customer-onboarding tracker that protects new-customer
  activation revenue — not a heavyweight CRM.
- **Tone:** clear, practical, founder-facing; calm and operational, not flashy.
- **Strategic principle:** every feature must serve one outcome — get a new customer from
  signup to active, and recover the ones who stall.

## Open assumptions (to confirm with the founder)

- Visual branding (logo, color palette) — indigo/amber is a working palette; the name
  "FirstMile" is set.
- Team size and weekly new-customer volume (needed to ground the North Star projection).
- Whether reminders are manually sent, scheduled, or both in v1.
